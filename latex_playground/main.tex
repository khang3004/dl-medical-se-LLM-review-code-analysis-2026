\begin{abstract}
This report presents a comprehensive critical review and engineering analysis of three state-of-the-art research articles presented at the International Conference on Computational Collective Intelligence (ICCCI 2025). The selected papers address key challenges at the intersection of artificial intelligence, clinical diagnostics, and automated software engineering: leukemia blast cell detection using YOLOv11s, Alzheimer's disease diagnosis using VGG16, and Large Language Model (LLM) code quality reviews. For each paper, we analyze the core methodology, evaluate the experimental results, and identify limitations. Moving beyond a simple literature review, we adopt the perspective of Senior Machine Learning Engineers and AI Architects to propose nine advanced technical extensions. These extensions—including self-supervised cytological pre-training, probabilistic boundary regression, 3D Swin Transformer voxel modeling, multi-modal cross-attention fusion, and execution-guided multi-agent debate protocols—provide concrete, mathematically formal frameworks designed to bridge the gap between academic research and production-grade deployment.
\end{abstract}
